I am an assistant professor of statistics at The University of Texas at Austin. I am with the Department of Information, Risk, and Operations Management (IROM) at the McCombs School of Business. I am also affiliated with the Division of Statistics and Scientific Computation (SSC).

I received my Ph.D. from Duke University in 2013, Master's from the Chinese Academy of Sciences in 2008, and B.Sc. from Nanjing University in 2005. My Ph.D. advisor was Lawrence Carin.

My research lies at the intersection of Bayesian statistics and machine learning. I am interested in developing statistical theory and methods, hierarchical models, and efficient Bayesian inference for big data. I am currently focused on the development of nonparametric Bayesian priors for both count-mixture modeling (clustering, mixed-membership modeling, topic modeling) and dictionary learning (feature learning, factor analysis).

I am building the negative binomial process family to introduce new exchangeable random partitions and novel clustering algorithms.


Research Highlights:

  • My recent research studies a new clustering framework whose number and sizes of clusters can be precisely described and flexibly controlled. I introduce the concept of a cluster structure to define a joint distribution of the sample size and its exchangeable random partitions. The cluster structure allows the probability distribution of the random partitions of a subset of the sample to be dependent on the sample size, a feature not permitted by Kingman's partition structure.
